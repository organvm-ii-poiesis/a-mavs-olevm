Whispers

Introspection and the quiet voices within

The Listener

There is a room inside the skull where echoes negotiate their shape. I am not the speaker here— merely the membrane, waiting.
Each thought arrives a stranger, bearing the weight of its own becoming, refusing the neat file of memory, choosing instead to drift, suspended between the heard and the said.
I listen to my own listening. Trapped in this recursive chamber where the voice that speaks is never quite the voice that hears it. The gap between them— that is where I live.
— interior geometry

Residue

What remains is not the event, but the dust of it— how light fell differently that morning, the particular silence before laughter, the name you didn't speak.
Memory is a palimpsest of omissions. We remember not what happened, but what we chose to feel about it, and even that, refined and filtered through the sediment of years.
I find myself in the margins, in the forgotten phone call, in the sentence I left unfinished, becoming more real than the actual moment, more solid than what occurred.
— sedimentary

Questions That Stay

Not all questions demand an answer. Some live best in the asking, in the space where certainty dissolves like sugar in silence.
I keep the unanswered ones close, the ones that ask who I am when no one is watching, what remains when the performance stops, whether the observer ever truly disappears.
These whispers are not seeking reply. They are the conversation I have with the version of myself that exists in the pause between breaths, in the threshold of the unnamed.
— persistent inquiry

The Catalogue of Selves

In the morning mirror: a stranger. By afternoon: someone you once knew. At dusk: a collection of gestures wearing the same face.
I am not one voice whispering. I am an archive of them, each demanding its turn, each convinced of its authenticity. The one who speaks at dawn goes silent by noon.
Which is the real whisper? The one that names itself? Or the ones that hide, insisting they are the truest all along?
— fragmented plurality

Listening Backwards

If I could rewind the voice, play it in reverse, would I find the silence underneath? The place where it was born, before it learned to make sound?
We speak forward only, always away from the quiet source. But the whisper knows a secret: it came from silence, and it returns there with every breath not taken.
Perhaps that is what I'm listening for— not the words, but the journey they're making back toward the unspoken, back toward the self that never needs to explain.
— sound in reverse
