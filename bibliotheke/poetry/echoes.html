Echoes

Time, recursion, and the loops we inhabit

Recursion Without End

I call myself, and I answer. I ask myself, and I reply. The distance between question and answer shrinks with each iteration until they collapse into the same breath, until the asker and the answered are indistinguishable.
There is a mirror somewhere reflecting a mirror reflecting a mirror into infinity. In each reflection a version of me grows smaller, fainter, but never quite disappears.
The deepest level of recursion is silence. Or perhaps it is another level down, impossible to reach, existing only in the intention to find it.
— stack overflow

The Tape Loop

Time is not a river. It is a loop of magnetic tape, running the same groove over and over. Each pass erases the previous one slightly, degrading the signal, adding noise, creating something new from the friction of repetition.
I have lived this moment before. I will live it again. Each time it changes imperceptibly, each time I am not quite the same person in not quite the same present.
The difference between memory and prophecy is merely which direction you're running the tape.
— feedback frequency

Identity as Echo

Who am I? The person who speaks, or the voice that returns? The original sound dies the moment it leaves the mouth. What persists is only the returning wave, the reflection, the echo of the echo, growing fainter and more distorted the farther it travels.
Perhaps identity is not a thing at all. Perhaps it is the process of listening to yourself bounce off the walls of circumstance, hearing how the world shapes what you say before it comes back to you unrecognizable.
The me that returns is not the me that departed. The echo never reaches the source— only approximates it, fading.
— acoustic displacement

The Museum of Selves

I keep the past versions of myself in a gallery— suspended behind glass, preserved in amber, frozen at the moment of their utterance. There is me-at-five, me-at-fifteen, me-at-twenty. Each one convinced they were the real version.
Who am I now, visiting them? Am I the sum of all these ghosts? Or am I already becoming another ghost, another relic to be viewed by whatever thing I'll become next?
The present tense is so brief. Already I'm in the museum, already I'm becoming a memory of what I thought I was.
— temporal curation

The Call That Never Ends

In mathematics there is a concept: the call stack. A function calls itself. Which calls itself. Which calls itself. Each layer is complete, self-contained, yet dependent on the layer below, which is dependent on the layer below that, descending forever into a point of infinite regress, where nothing is ever truly resolved.
I exist at every level of this stack simultaneously. I wait for myself to finish so I can finish. So I can finish. So I can finish.
The stack never clears. There is no base case. There is only the echo calling to the echo calling to the echo into silence.
— infinite recursion
