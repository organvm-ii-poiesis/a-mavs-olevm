Design Practice & Projects

ETCETER4 Website

Multi-year web-based project serving as both portfolio and experimental space. Features non-linear navigation, generative systems, and integration of music, writing, and visual work.

2010-present View Project →

Initiated in 2010 as a portfolio site, evolved into an experimental web labyrinth. Went dormant in 2016-2017, with most content commented out. Revived in 2025 with expanded architectural vision incorporating 15 "chambers" inspired by classical architecture.

Technologies: HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, P5.js Design influences: Radiohead (Kid A era), web brutalism Architecture: Five Pillars + Pantheon expansion

Concept: Challenges conventional portfolio structure by embracing non-linear, exploratory navigation inspired by the metaphor of a labyrinth. Technical Approach: Hash-based routing, generative visuals, self-hosted media infrastructure. Significance: Demonstrates integration of design thinking, technical implementation, and conceptual art practice.

Loophole (2016)

Interactive web-based text piece exploring repetition, fragmentation, and digital reading. Glitched typography and non-linear navigation.

Web-based artwork 2016 Experience →

Created during transition period when much of the ETCETER4 site was being commented out. Represents an interest in web-native poetics and the affordances of HTML/CSS for experimental writing. Influenced by net.art practices of the 1990s-2000s.

Created: 2016 Technologies: HTML, CSS, JavaScript Form: Interactive hypertext

Conceptual Framework: Interrogates the "loop" as both technical structure (in code) and metaphor for compulsive reading/clicking behaviors. Medium-Specificity: Exploits web-specific affordances that wouldn't translate to print or other media. Lineage: Positions itself within traditions of concrete poetry, net.art, and electronic literature.
