Between Worlds

02 / 02 / 2026

Creative work exists in a liminal space—neither fully of the maker nor separate from them. When I'm deep in a project, I become something other. My concerns shift, my priorities realign. The real world grows distant, almost irrelevant. All that matters is the work, the vision, the compulsion to manifest something that doesn't yet exist.

This state used to frighten me. I thought it meant I was unhealthy, obsessive, disconnected. But I'm learning to honor it. This is where the real work happens—in that space between my conscious self and whatever wants to be born through me. It's not escapism. It's communion. It's the place where growth becomes possible.

I've noticed something curious: the more I surrender to the creative process, the more I understand myself. It's paradoxical. By stepping outside myself into a vision, I come closer to knowing who I actually am. Maybe that's because creation is a form of self-discovery. Every project teaches me something essential about my values, my obsessions, my deepest desires.

There's also a strange responsibility in this space. When you're living between worlds, you carry something fragile. An idea in formation, a vision not yet realized. You have to protect it. You have to honor the vulnerability of not-yet-knowingness. But you also have to be willing to fail. To create something imperfect and release it anyway, because perfectionism is just fear wearing a fancy name.

I think personal growth and creative work are inseparable. Every person I'm becoming requires a new version of my craft. Every obstacle I overcome teaches me something about art-making, about patience, about the courage it takes to be honest. The two feed each other in ways I'm only beginning to understand.

Today, I'm grateful for this between-space. For the permission it gives me to be someone other than I was yesterday. For the way it keeps me reaching toward what I could become.
