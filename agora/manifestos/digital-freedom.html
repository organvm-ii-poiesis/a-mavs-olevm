MANIFESTO

Digital Freedom

The right to an open, decentralized, liberated web

We stand at a precipice. The digital commons—once promised as humanity's great equalizer—has become a walled garden of corporate surveillance and algorithmic control. The time for quiet negotiation has passed. The time for action is now.
1

Data is a Human Right

Every person's digital existence—their thoughts, movements, relationships, desires—belongs to themselves alone. No corporation, no algorithm, no state apparatus shall harvest human data without explicit, informed, revocable consent. Data collected is data that enslaves. Freedom begins with sovereignty over one's own information.

2

Decentralization Is Non-Negotiable

Centralized platforms are inherent instruments of control. They concentrate power, enable censorship, and render users powerless. We demand a web built on distributed protocols—where authority is spread, decision-making is decentralized, and no single entity can dictate the rules of participation. Decentralization is not a technical preference; it is a political imperative.

3

Code Must Be Free

Open-source software is the beating heart of digital liberation. Proprietary systems hide their logic, their assumptions, their biases. We demand transparency. Code that governs our lives must be inspectable, auditable, and modifiable by those it affects. Freedom requires the ability to see how you are being controlled.

4

Interoperability Over Monopoly

Walled gardens strangle innovation and entrap users. Interoperable systems allow choice, movement, and freedom. We reject lock-in. We demand standards that enable users to move their data, their identity, their connections freely between services. Digital freedom dies in silos.

5

Encryption Is Your Shield

End-to-end encryption is not a luxury. It is a necessity. It is a right. Those who would weaken encryption in the name of security are tyrants dressed in the language of order. We defend encryption absolutely. Your private communications must remain private—from corporations, from governments, from all who would surveil without consent.

6

Right to Anonymity, Right to Identity

Some speak online from positions of safety. Others speak at great risk. We protect the right to anonymous speech—to those who need it. We also protect the right to verified identity—for those who choose it. Digital freedom means choice in how you present yourself to the world.

7

Resist Algorithmic Capture

Algorithms are not neutral. They embed the values and interests of those who design them. We demand algorithmic transparency and the right to opt out of algorithmic mediation entirely. Your attention is not a commodity. Your behavior is not data to be mined. Resist the algorithm. Choose your own feed.

8

Access Is Justice

Digital freedom is hollow for those without connection. We demand universal access to the internet as a foundational right. We demand affordable technology. We demand tools that work across devices, across bandwidths, across barriers. Freedom on the internet means nothing if the internet itself is unreachable.

9

Regulation Must Protect, Not Control

Government involvement in the internet must serve one purpose: protecting freedom. Regulation that weakens encryption, mandates backdoors, enables surveillance, or suppresses speech is not protection—it is oppression. Good regulation breaks monopolies, protects privacy, and ensures access. Bad regulation chains freedom itself.

10

This Is Only the Beginning

Digital freedom is not a destination. It is a constant struggle against forces that profit from control. We commit to building alternatives. We commit to organizing. We commit to defending these principles with code, with community, with culture. The open web is not given—it is seized by those who refuse to be ruled.

We are the architects of our own digital liberation.
We are building the future, brick by encrypted brick.
Join us.
— The Agora, in assembly
For a web that belongs to all of us
